Learning Practice v2.1

Scientific Method

Resource: Martin Seligman’s What you can change and what you can’t - the chapter on “Growing up - at last”.

Aim: Extract the causal model from a section

Correlations

easy (not hard), childhood events hold sway over what kind of adult you become (what kind of variables are these? binary - healthy and unhealthy?)

evidence seems to be right in front of your eyes

smart parents, smart kids, books and conversations

broken homes, kids often divorce, not good role models

sexually abused kids, frightened pessimists, found the world a frightful place

observations, confounded by other variables, specifically genes

genetic interpretation seems farfetched while the childhood interpretation sounds so comfortably true

Freud’s theory about catharsis should sound familiar because the premises are the same of the inner-child movement.

Steady decline in academia, but movies, TV, and the general public still love them.

inner child movement, most popular self-help movement of the 90s

Childhood trauma and catharsis make good theatre, which causes part of the appeal.

Nazis used genetics to do evil; so, anything they used was tainted.

American psychology was already environmental and now became wedded to childhood personality and the dogma of human plasticity

Being a victim, blaming someone else or the system consoles us; being a failure does not. So, we’ll believe a victimhood theory?

Evidence: AA shifted the blame for drinking too much from personal fault to the disease of alcoholism. This was an intervention and the explanation became popular, suggesting that more consoling theories are accepted more.

Evidence: The explanation for underperformance shifted from “stupidity” and “laziness” to “discrimination”.

The old explanations were pessimistic (personal, pervasive, and permanent) and thus made you feel helpless.

Personal troubles are caused not by lasting personal sins but by childhood mistreatment.

Lesson

What does this forbid? “Evidence seems to be right in front of your eyes” means that it isn’t hidden or missing.
