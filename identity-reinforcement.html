Reinforcing your Identity

Reinforcement

Hypothesis: Your identity is made of a bunch of rules that get strengthened or weakened by your thoughts and actions and by rewards and punishments.

If you see people mock those who bomb on stage, you will learn the rule that bombing on stage is worthy of disrespect. And if you fear that you are a bad public speaker, you will feel unworthy yourself and avoid all public-speaking occasions.

How to shape your identity? Here are three of my core hypotheses:

First, decide your identity. What do you want to respect and disrespect? Helpful question: what’s your current identity?

Second, reinforce only those rules and punish all others. See everything through the lens of your desired identity and act in line with it.

Lastly, realize that undesirable behaviour is due to a weak identity. So, strengthen your true identity.

Give solid examples for all of the above. What evidence convinced me?

How do people with different identities differ in their behaviour? What is their reinforcement or punishment?

What about different domains? What do they reinforce or punish? Men vs women? What if you took a “women’s bag” or wore a pair of “women’s sunglasses”?

There’s money, sex, etc.
