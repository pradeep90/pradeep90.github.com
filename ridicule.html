Ridicule

Ridicule

Hypothesis: That’s the major form of punishment.

Hypothesis: Shaming and ridicule defines the box within which you live your life.

And ridicule is not all bad. Ridicule is what stops us from shooting ourselves in the foot in any new domain. It’s how we keep people in line.

For example, in intellectual forums like LessWrong, spelling and grammatical errors are frowned upon, even in the comments. Why? Because writing an error-free comment shows that you’ve thought through your comment, looked it over a couple of times, and respected others’ time. These are desirable things. If you wanted hastily put-together, mistake-ridden prose, you would go on to any other forum of the internet. By ridiculing people who write in SMS-speak, the forum maintains a high standard of commenting.

In cricket, we mock batsmen who get out for a duck. A golden duck is worst of all - they used to show graphics of a duck walking out dejected on the big screen, set to comical music. The reason a duck is so bad is because it means you haven’t showed even the minimum amount of application needed to stay at the crease. Forget about fours and sixes; you couldn’t even tap the ball and run for a quick single. That’s not what the team wants. Hence the ridicule.

Happiness for Free

Observation: Experts in some field may tend to look down on those who have fun from “cheap” or “unworthy” sources like mass comedy movies or going out to tourist spots or whatever.

Hypothesis: Those people are getting fun from stuff that is outside the experts’ identity. They’re getting rewards for free. Not cool. That hardly leaves any incentive to devote their every waking moment to their identity.

(I’m not saying I agree with this. Just that this is what I observe.)
