Scientific Method Test v1.2

Scientific Method v1.2

Let’s continue using my v1.0 algorithm: Look at each paragraph and get the causal link.

At each step, ask if it is a correlation or a cause. In the first pass, identify the correlations. Then, investigate causes. Use time and space to figure out the direction of causality. Finally, for every top-level variable, ask what its cause is.

Mission: Use this on one PG essay.

Time: 1 hour

(Note: A -> B stands for “A causes B”)

Filters that Fight Back by PG

Correlations

follow links in mails, improve accuracy of Bayesian spam filters

Some guy did it only in borderline cases. “Why only borderline cases?”, asks PG. Also, “why only once?”

following all mails, amusing side-effect

popular mail clients do this, spammer’s servers take severe pounding

attack, very highly targeted (high precision?)

no other attack, as highly targeted

punish mode, spider every link n times

current email system, too passive (?)

suggestions to fix the problem of passiveness, new protocols; this one, not so

huge volume of spam, so far worked in favour of spammer (higher chances of clicks); now, work against spammer (higher load on servers)

auto-retrieving spam filters, drive spammer’s costs up, sales down; bandwidth goes up like hell, servers halt, unavailable to gullible customers

rebound filters, want it only for suspected spammers (want very low false positives)

url sent to millions of people, spam url

submitting every http request in every email, fine nearly all the time (low false positives)

urls from free email services, not fine (false positive)

auto-retrieval + blacklists of spamvertized sites, protect legitimate sites (number of times legitimate sites are crawled), prevent abuse (people spam with legitimate sites’ urls to get them DoS’d)

sites on blacklist, alone crawled (% of crawled sites from blacklist)

site blacklisted, humans inspection

lifetime of spam at least several hours, easy to update a list, interfere with promotion of new site (how many people see the spam or visit site)

high-bandwidth connections (bandwidth), high-volume auto-retrieval only practical for them (cost per mail)

enough of such people, serious trouble (load on spam servers)

in order to reach a few gullible people (target %), spam everyone (volume)

non-gullible people (people with low #clicks on spam), collateral damage (not target, but still paying in time)

non-gullible people stop (or threaten to stop) gullible from responding (somehow decrease % gullible clicks), non-gullible people stop getting spam (amount of spam received by non-gullible people; what about spam received by gullible people?)

auto-retrieving spam filters, stop (or threaten to stop) gullible people from responding to spam (decrease #gullible clicks)

auto-retrieving spam filter, kill spam (#total spam goes to zero)? Not quite

biggest spammers (highest spam volume times number of mails), protect their servers against auto-retrieving filters (somehow not increase server load due to auto-retrieving filters)

easiest and cheapest way to protect their servers (cost, ease of implementation and maintenance), working unsubscribe links (boolean)

working unsubscribe links, necessity for smaller fry (i.e., if they didn’t do that, they would get high server loads)

auto-retrieving filters become common (#filter users), auto-unsubscribing filters (% of non-unsubscribed spam mails)

people who don’t use the right software, spam (along with OS crashes, viruses, popups)

Conclusions

Ensure that each part of your correlation or cause is a variable. Don’t deal with abstract nonsense. Operationalize, in other words.
