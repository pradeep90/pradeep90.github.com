Scientific Method Test v1.2

Scientific Method v1.2

Let’s continue using my v1.0 algorithm: Look at each paragraph and get the causal link.

At each step, ask if it is a correlation or a cause. In the first pass, identify the correlations. Then, investigate causes. Use time and space to figure out the direction of causality. Finally, for every top-level variable, ask what its cause is.

Mission: Use this on one PG essay.

Time: 1 hour

(Note: A -> B stands for “A causes B”)

Filters that Fight Back by PG

Correlations and Causes

follow links in mails -> improve accuracy of Bayesian spam filters

Some guy did it only in borderline cases. once per borderline case -> improvement; “Why only borderline cases?”, asks PG. Also, “why only once?”

following all mails -> amusing side-effect (server load of spammer)

popular mail clients do this (#people who do auto-retrieval) -> spammer’s servers take severe pounding (server load)

auto-retrieval -> very highly targeted (high precision and high recall? you attack just the spammers)

other attacks -> not as highly targeted

punish mode = spider every link n times

current email system, too passive (?)

suggestions to fix the problem of passiveness, new protocols; this one, not so. new protocols + changes -> fix some problems with email;

huge volume of spam, so far worked in favour of spammer (higher chances of clicks); now, work against spammer (higher load on servers). Huge volume -> higher chances of clicks; now, huge volume, high #auto-retrieval clients -> high server loads

auto-retrieving spam filters, drive spammer’s costs up, sales down; bandwidth goes up like hell, servers halt, unavailable to gullible customers; auto-retrieving spam filters -> #server requests -> bandwidth goes up, you don’t pay extra -> servers halt -> unavailable to gullible customers

rebound filters, want it only for suspected spammers (want very low false positives). low false positives = legitimate sites not affected

url sent to millions of people, spam url; spam url -> sent to millions of people

submitting every http request in every email, fine nearly all the time (low false positives); submit every http request -> low false positives

urls from free email services, not fine (false positive); false positives -> bad

auto-retrieval + blacklists of spamvertized sites, protect legitimate sites (number of times legitimate sites are crawled), prevent abuse (people spam with legitimate sites’ urls to get them DoS’d). auto-retrieval, blacklist -> crawl legitimate sites few times, don’t get fooled by spammers sending legitimate urls as a hoax

sites on blacklist, alone crawled (% of crawled sites from blacklist); blacklist -> % of sites crawled not from blacklist

site blacklisted, humans inspection; human inspection of url -> url in blacklist

lifetime of spam at least several hours, easy to update a list, interfere with promotion of new site (how many people see the spam or visit site); lifetime of span -> how many people see spam for newly promoted sites

high-bandwidth connections (bandwidth), high-volume auto-retrieval only practical for them (cost per mail); bandwidth -> cost of auto-retrieval system

enough of such people, serious trouble (load on spam servers); #people -> load on spam servers

in order to reach a few gullible people (target %), spam everyone (volume); volume -> #clicks

non-gullible people (people with low #clicks on spam), collateral damage (not target, but still paying in time); spam, non-clickers -> no profit to spammers, loss in time to the non-clickers; Basically, click -> profit to spammers; so, no click -> no profit.

non-gullible people stop (or threaten to stop) gullible from responding (somehow decrease % gullible clicks), non-gullible people stop getting spam (amount of spam received by non-gullible people; what about spam received by gullible people?). decrease #clicks on spam -> decrease amount of spam received by non-clickers (how?)

auto-retrieving spam filters, stop (or threaten to stop) gullible people from responding to spam (decrease #gullible clicks); auto-retrieving spam filters -> decrease #clicks on spam

auto-retrieving spam filter, kill spam (#total spam goes to zero)? Not quite; auto-retrieving spam filters -> amount of spam (will not go to zero)

biggest spammers (highest spam volume times number of mails), protect their servers against auto-retrieving filters (somehow not increase server load due to auto-retrieving filters); biggest spammers -> somehow not increase server load despite filters

easiest and cheapest way to protect their servers (cost, ease of implementation and maintenance), working unsubscribe links (boolean); unsubscribe links -> auto-retrieval filters unsubscribe automatically -> server load is not increased

working unsubscribe links, necessity for smaller fry (i.e., if they didn’t do that, they would get high server loads); smaller fry, didn’t have working unsubscribe links -> high server loads

auto-retrieving filters become common (#filter users), auto-unsubscribing filters (% of non-unsubscribed spam mails); #filter users -> decrease #non-unsubscribed spam

people who don’t use the right software -> spam (along with OS crashes, viruses, popups)

Summary

Key variables: amount of spam for non-clickers, cost to non-clickers

Counter-attack: number of people with auto-retrieval filters -> server load of spammers, high precision and recall, no new protocols; server load -> bandwidth goes up -> servers halt -> unavailable to gullible customers

Low false positives: human inspection -> blacklist, plus auto-retrieval -> crawl legitimate sites very few times, prevent abuse;

Automatic unsubscribing: working unsubscribe links -> auto-retrieval filters unsubscribe automatically -> server load is not increased, unsubscribed from spam

Freedom for the non-gullible: #clicks by gullible -> spam -> no profit to spammers from non-clickers, loss of time for non-clickers; auto-retrieval filters -> DoS attack on spam servers -> decrease #clicks by gullible -> drive out spammers or become unsubscribed automatically -> decrease spam for non-clickers

Conclusions

Ensure that each part of your correlation or cause is a variable. Don’t deal with abstract nonsense. Operationalize, in other words.

For the causal links you posit, ask how the cause leads to the effect.

How to test or understand causal structures? Take each causal link in isolation; because of locality of causality, it should be enough to explain the effect. You should need no other information.
