Praise

Praise

Conversely, people applaud those batsmen who managed to get a hundred. There were probably half a dozen times where they could have lofted the ball in the air or nicked one to the keeper, but they didn’t. They hung in, kept their head even after getting a fifty, navigated the nervous nineties, and got to the century. All those things are desirable for the team and spectators. Hence the praise.

Hypothesis: Praise is the major form of reinforcement.

Other people’s appreciation is powerful and rare.

Compliments are powerful.
