Pain in the Hands

Note: This is gonna be a short post, for obvious reasons.

History

I’ve been having a lot of pain in my hands over the last year.

Actually, I’ve been having pain in my hands while typing for a long time now. Yes, I’m dumb and I should have sorted this shit out back when it started. Those are Sunk Costs now. Let me at least fix the problem now.

How long?

I first felt pain in my wrists seven years ago, when I was in the eleventh standard. That is why I started using a different, more ergonomic keyboard layout - the Dvorak layout.

I didn’t have too much pain after that, but I was always wary of the pain returning. I went to college for a degree in Computer Science. Lots of programming. Such typing. Much pain.

At each point, I suspected different things. I thought it was because I was typing a lot. But then, so were a lot of the other people. Why weren’t they feeling pain?

I then felt I was resting my wrists on the keyboard while typing. So, I tried to stop doing that. Dunno if I fully succeeded, because that shit happens unconsciously.

At some point after my second year in college, I wasn’t able to use the mouse without feeling pain. I did everything using keyboard shortcuts because using the mouse increased my discomfort. Using my laptop’s mousepad was the worst of all - it hurt badly.

Midway through my final year, the pain kinda became a constant. It was at its peak when I did intense programming, say before a project deadline. But it was there all the time. I had trouble writing the exams. Writing with a pen or pencil hurt way worse than typing. I guess gripping the pen and making it move finely stressed my hands a lot.

Geography

It hurts in the middle of my palms. Then, the thumb muscle (the part below the thumb). Then, the base of each finger - it hurts there the way it hurts after you write a really long exam paper.

Recently, it is hurting at the base of my wrists, the place where my hand and wrists are joined. And then, a third of the way down the hand… it hurts like there’s a nerve in there which is throbbing. I feel like massaging that place to relieve the pain.

Plus, it hurts at the tips of my fingers.

Advice

Patient: Doctor, it hurts when I move my hand this way.

Doctor: Well, then, don’t do that.

I don’t think I can stop typing anytime soon.

I need to type a lot at work. I’m working as a programmer right now. So, I’m at my desk for around 8 hours everyday.

Plus, all the important stuff I want to do in life - coming up with ideas, writing essays, writing programs, whatever… everything depends on me typing like hell everything seems to depend on me typing like hell.

It’s personal. Not.

I kept thinking that I have pain in my hands because I’m doing something totally wrong, like there is something uniquely broken about me. It felt like I deserved the pain or something. Like, the more of a dumbass you are, the more pain you will keep getting.

I’m not so sure of that anymore.

It was only towards the final year of college that I began to notice similar symptoms among my classmates. There was a guy who used to flex his hands every so often while playing Quake using the mouse. I asked him why and he said he was feeling some discomfort. He wasn’t too worried about it.

Some other guys were looking out for solutions to the pain in their hands.

I started getting the feeling that I am not alone.

Cause

What do I think are the causes of this pain?

I don’t know for sure.

What increases the pain?

What decreases the pain?

Is there a cure?

Lots more on this coming soon.

Solution

I don’t know the best way forward.

But if there is a way for me to get rid of this pain and stop it from ruining my life, I’m gonna find it. And take action on it.

This is the most important problem I’m facing right now. It has been elevated to top priority. Pain has a way of making that happen.
