What is Confusion?

Entry Question: What is Confusion?

What is Confusion?

Wait. What is Confusion?

A better question: How are you different when you are Confused from when you are not Confused?

When you don’t know a topic well, when you have a wrong model, you won’t make accurate predictions. You will either make wrong predictions or just random predictions. By itself, that’s not a problem. There’s a lot of stuff out there. You can’t expect to know everything (right away).

The problem comes when you don’t notice that you’re wrong or that you aren’t making useful predictions. You don’t get surprised by those outcomes even though you should.

So, confused people don’t notice when they’re wrong. Confused people don’t notice when they have a useless model - one that doesn’t constrain anticipation.

Will the confusion go away if I point it out to you explicitly? Will you then be able to improve your model?

That’s the second Confusion punch. When you’re confused, you aren’t able to improve your models no matter how much evidence you come across. (Really? Even after a lot of evidence? Won’t you at least have ad hoc rules like “phlogiston” -> fire? Won’t that give you some predictive power)

It is what keeps you from actually solving the problem. It will make you feel that solving the problem is hopeless.

It is what stops you from knowing the cause of some particular outcome and, thus, keeps you from fixing your problems.

Confusion is your Worst Nightmare!

Ok. So, that is what Confusion looks like. But what is it like on the inside? What is actually happening here? How can we fix it?

(Am I confused about Confusion? Can I make predictions about it? Notice surprise about it? Update on evidence? Feel that there exists a solution? Describe the cause for each of the behaviour?)

No Advance Predictions

All your predictions will be based on stuff you’ve seen

No useful novel computations on your own.

Phlogiston.

The Road Ahead

Ok, SPK. You have me convinced. Confusion is the root of all evil. How do we get rid of Confusion?
