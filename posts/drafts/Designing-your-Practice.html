Goals

Entry question: What do we really want from Deliberate Practice?

Essay Status: In progress.

Skill

Skill is nothing but getting some kind of Impact in a particular way.

The skill of hitting cover drives is about hitting the ball through the cover region (Impact) by playing in the V (the way to do it).

Piano skill is about creating the right sounds at the right time from a piano (Impact) by pressing the right keys (the way to do it). The music is what matters.

Programming skill is about creating the right kind of programs - working, readable, extensible, etc (Impact) by writing it all out by yourself and your editor (the way to do it).

Skill level

What does it mean to say that you have a certain skill level in a field?

Answer: You are consistently able to get a certain amount of Impact (in the way that the skill specifies).

Example: If you consider batsmanship to be the skill of getting hundreds and having a high average and doing it with a high strike rate, then Sachin has high skill as a batsman. Sachin is able to get lots of hundreds, lots of runs, high average, decent strike rate, etc. He is able to get the Impact we want from a batsman (as defined above).

It makes no sense to talk about “skill level” in isolation without mentioning the Impact you want from it. “Sachin is highly skilled” - some guy says this, the other guy disagrees, and they end up arguing for hours. Yo! What do you mean “highly skilled”? Define the Impact you expect and then fight.

Skill and Impact

We need to distinguish between the Skill and the Impact we want from that Skill. You do different things to get each one.

To get more Impact, you use the relevant Skill. While batting, you want to score boundaries. So, you use the Skill of “cover drive” to score boundaries. You want to keep scoring boundaries, you keep using your “cover drive” Skill (among others).

To get more Skill, you use Deliberate Practice. Before the match, you want to improve your “cover drive” Skill. So, you practice it rigorously, using exercises designed to increase your performance (hitting the middle of the bat, hitting in the gap, hitting along the ground) with tight feedback and lots of repetitions.

What do you want to practice?

Okay. Now that we about Skill and the distinction between Skill and Impact, what are the Skills we need to master?

Let’s take a look back at our Big Problems.

Truth-finding is a skill. And a very important one, as we have seen. We definitely need to improve our performance in this skill.

Generating Motivation for some activity is another skill. If we agree that Akrasia is one of the main bottlenecks in getting the Impact we want (I’ll talk about the other bottleneck soon), then we need to become much better at the skill of generating Motivation.

Narrowing it Down

Is that all? We just named two things and called them “skills”. Should we just go ahead and practice them?

Wait a minute. Skill is defined by the Impact you want and the way you want to do it.

This was why I had spent the past couple of months being utterly confused about what to do with these two “skills”. I used a vague, implicit definition in my head without talking about the actual Impact I wanted.

If you are a master at “Truth-finding”, then, well, you should be able to discover the truth about anything, anywhere, anytime. Wow, that would be a handy skill. That would also be an insanely difficult skill to get in any reasonable time frame. It’s too broad. It’s daunting. I wouldn’t even know where to begin. On one hand, I would need great Impact from the skill. No Impact, No Right to Talk. On the other hand, I would need to learn and practice truly general techniques that can apply to any subject under the sun, not just math or economics or whatever.

If you had a fast computer for a brain, you could just apply (a restricted form of) Bayes Theorem over and over till you got to epic Predictive Power. But you don’t. Even within a single field, if you want to get great results, you need to learn very complex techniques that compensate for your brain’s fallibility and slowness. Just look at the advanced journals or textbooks in any major field. It is not a piece of cake. And if you want to get great results across many fields, with the aforementioned fallible and slow human brain, good lord, you’re screwed. I don’t know of any current techniques that can do the trick. The current Art of Rationality isn’t all that effective.

Which means, if a tractable solution is possible, we have to discover it anew. That is a tremendous challenge. Not happening anytime soon, not with the current level of human thinking (raw brainpower and methods of reasoning). No wonder I was stymied.

We need to restrict our scope, at least at the beginning. Start out with a smaller goal, get to it, and then see where to go from there. Instead of looking for awesomeness across all fields in general, let’s shoot for decent power in a couple of specific fields.

It’s not optional

We would strictly be making a mistake if we went off and wasted time on other things. Time is scarce, especially time in your 20s. You have to narrow down the most essential skills.

What does this not include? Getting to the truth about random stuff that is happening somewhere - would be cool, but it’s not on our menu. Discovering solutions to any problems that happen in your life - nope. Too broad. Not worth it.

We’re being narrow in our focus. We give up on these nice-to-have-but-not-worth-it Impacts, and focus on a few specific super-High-Value Impacts.

Motivation

What is the Impact I seek here?

  • I want to be able to study hard to get results in the fields above.

    Techniques are useless without the Motivation to stick to the problems and see things through to the end.

  • I want to have the motivation to do lots of original thinking

    I need to take the effort to understand stuff without just parroting it, and discover new things on my own. I need to have the desire to see things with fresh eyes and work for it.

  • I want to be able to do the uncomfortable and maybe tedious tasks required for the money-making stuff, if it comes to that

    This might include networking, research, legwork, etc.

  • I want to be able to do tons of hardcore Deliberate Practice

    Ideas alone are worthless. You need to put in the effort to grow your skill.

  • I need to be able to stay focussed on these tasks and not get distracted by crap

    This is, imho, the biggest failure mode. These are the termites that eat away at your plans from the inside.

  • I want to take action on stuff that I absolutely need to do but don’t feel like doing

    There are several biases (like Scope Insensitivity, etc.) that keep us from feeling that something is important, even though it is. I need to feel motivation for doing these crucial things, else there is no point to the rest of my actions. I would have won the battles but lost the war.

    In other words, I need to feel motivation for actions that are even more boring than “saving for retirement” (who wants to do that? boring!) or “flossing everyday”. This is a hard problem.

So, that’s it. Motivation for doing truckloads of Deliberate Practice, hard study, original thinking, working outside of your Comfort zone, and doing What Needs to be Done. Plus, Keeping your Focus.

What is not included above? Being able to do any activity no matter how boring it is. Nope. Being able to do all the items on your To-Do List (should be doable for a true master) - but nope. Being able to do all the errands I procrastinate on - nope. We let the dishes pile up if need be, we tolerate slightly poor exercise regimens.

We are not looking for all-round Increase-Motivation-for-Anything powers here. Just the power to increase motivation for a few things we care about.

Identifying Skills

So how do we identify the skills that will get us the Impact we want?

Case Study: Cricket Batsman

Desired Impact: Want to hit boundaries.

Breaking it down: We can hit boundaries on the off-side, leg-side, behind the wicketkeeper, and straight down the ground.

Skills:

What skills can I use to hit boundaries on the off-side? Cover drive, cut shot, etc.

What skills can I use to hit boundaries on the leg-side? Pull, hook, sweep, etc.

Behind the wicketkeeper? Dil-scoop, upar cut, etc.

Straight down the ground? Straight drive and so on.

Cool. We now have to Deliberate Practice each of these skills in isolation. Done.

Reverse-Engineer and Re-Create

Once you break the big-ass Impact into specific little Impacts, you can easily identify the skills that can get you there.

Look at people who are great at getting that Impact and then tease out the specific skills they use to get it. Identify the masters and ask - How did they do it?

For example, Ganguly gets a lot of his boundaries on the off-side. How does he do it? Lots of cuts and cover drives. Cool. I’ll have some of that. Sachin gets lots of fours down the ground. How does he do it? Straight drive. Awesome.

The point is to reverse-engineer the skills of the experts so well that, given enough time and data and resources, you should be able to re-create their performance. You should be able to get to their skill level and get the results they get. That is the true test for whether you have captured the essence of their mastery.

For the sciences, say, your aim is to get such a good grasp of their skills so that, given the data or resources that the scientists in that field possess, you should be able to derive all the important results they have got. For example, if you care about physics, then you should get such a good grasp of the scientific method that you would be able to derive stuff like gravity, laws of motion, thermodynamics, etc. from the necessary data without peeking at the textbook derivation.

If that seems daunting or impossible, we have to put things in historical perspective - everything was a brand-new mystery once upon a time. There was nothing magical about what Newton did or what Galileo did or what Einstein did. We live in a Merely Real World - no magic. All the mystery is in our heads. They applied some specific skills to get the results they got. Maybe the skills are too complex or too resource-intensive for us average people to master, but we need to accept first that they are just skills. It is doable in theory.

Let’s try to reverse-engineer the skills for Truth and Motivation.

Real-World Tests

Now, I can use these as Real-World Tests for my skills. If at any time, I want to see just how much I have progressed, I can take on a mini-challenge from these areas and see how well I’ve done. It’s the equivalent of taking a practical exam in some course.

Design and Implementation

We want Design and Implementation to evolve together. Just like language developers develop a language alongside a major project in that language. That way you can discover what you really need to have in the language and can keep changing it with the aim of making it a better language for that project.

It’s the same way with a Skill and a Project. Try to do the project using the current skill. See where it is lacking. Go and patch the skill so that that flaw is fixed. Rinse and repeat.

We can pick interesting mini-projects from these domains and use them to guide our skill development.

Getting practical feedback

We want cheap and quick feedback if want to use it in Deliberate Practice.

The Most Important Requirement

Feedback.

Without this, everything else is useless. You can have the most brilliant ideas about how to practice the shit out of some skill, but it will all go to nought if you don’t have feedback.

You need feedback to keep yourself motivated, first and foremost. Remember that “Delay” factor in the denominator of the Motivation Equation? When you don’t get feedback right away, the Delay is very high. Which means, your Motivation is very low. Which means you ain’t gonna be doing much more of that activity.

Next, feedback is crucial for information. You need to know whether you’re doing it right or not. Should you keep doing it this way or should you change it a bit? How should you change it? How much should you change it? You need feedback for this.

Concretely Speaking

What exactly do I mean by Feedback?

I mean some sort of measurement of our performance. We need something that gives us quick feedback, hopefully instantly and continuously.

Let’s look at the problem in more detail.

What are some examples of feedback mechanisms that people use during practice?

Bowlers practicing in the nets can see where the ball pitched and by how much it missed the stumps.

Batsman practicing in the nets can see whether they hit the ball or not, and can feel where they hit it on the bat, and which direction it went in.

People practicing an instrument have a feel in their head for the piece they are trying to play. They have heard the original before and are comparing their own performance against the tune in their head.
