Problems with the normal approach

You’re demanding too much of yourself.

We need to go a bit easy on ourselves.

We want to write, but are being distracted by other, easier stuff that’s more enjoyable.

If you put too much of an ask on yourself, you’re gonna fail inevitably. And then, you’re gonna beat yourself up about it.

Does that mean we give up?

We NEVER give up (unless it’s impossible, in which case we give up as soon as possible).

The NaNoWriMo solution

For the clueless, NaNoWriMo is National Novel Writing Month. Come November, people sharpen their pencils and oil their keyboards to get going towards their target - 50,000 words in 30 days.

Why would anybody do that? Don’t they know that writing novels takes about three and a half years and that you only attempt to do so if you have a lifetime’s worth of interesting experiences and an inexplicable divine gift and/or an idea about a magic school (Love you, JKR!)?

Many of them get it done, though. In spades. I’ve heard of novels being written of the order of hundreds of thousands of words (that’s like 2-3 mainstream-size novels, in 30 days).

The project started in July 1999 with just 21 participants, but by the 2010 event over 200,000 people took part - writing a total of over 2.8 billion words.

– http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Novel_Writing_Month

Cool. I’m convinced. What’s their secret?

There’s a lot of reasons to their success, but the one that comes straight to mind is this - Focus on Quantity over Quality.

SPK’s solution for all the non-novelists out there

  • Step 1: Start a blog

    Doesn’t have to be anything fancy. You could go Blogspot. Or Wordpress. Or you could go Hakyll!

  • Step 2: Write 300 words. Everyday.

    End of story.

  • Step 3: Write a post gloating about your new idea for getting over Writer’s Block

    Heh.

You don’t have to write a complete post every day. Who says you have to put out complete, packaged posts with a ribbon on them?

Write 300 words in any order you want. Throw a few words at the beginning. A couple at the end. Maybe a dozen words in a paragraph here, a baker’s dozen in a paragraph there. A quote or two from your favourite authors and hey! You’re done for the day!

You can then go back to your village and say proudly, “Naanum oru Writer, da!”

Feel free to come back some other time and add some more words, maybe cleaning up a few of your earlier sentences. Throw in a snappy introduction and a good conclusion and you have a complete post.

Doesn’t it sound ridiculously easy?

Perhaps that’s the answer to my question earlier. Why might this work so effectively?

When Quantity comes, can Quality be far behind?

– Robert Forst, yesterday
