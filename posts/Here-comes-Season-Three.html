Here comes... Season Three

Entry question: Why didn’t I write anything for the last six months? Rhetorical.

Essays online vs Notes offline

What’s the difference?

Just as inviting people over forces you to clean up your apartment, writing something that other people will read forces you to think well. So it does matter to have an audience. The things I’ve written just for myself are no good. They tend to peter out. When I run into difficulties, I find I conclude with a few vague questions and then drift off to get a cup of tea.

– Paul Graham, The Age of the Essay

Amen. Can’t put it better than that mofo.

I was fooling myself these last few months by capturing all my thoughts in Org-mode notes on my computer. I racked up a huge stack of words in those notes (more than 33,000 words) and I did feel I had a lot of interesting ideas in there. But, that is nowhere near as effective as an essay.

Most of those “ideas” are just short headings with 50-100 words of exposition. That hardly pushes me to flesh it out and talk about the consequences for my life. I’m not forced to connect it to my existing knowledge. I don’t have to present it to a neutral reader and specify all my unspoken assumptions. It’s just some piece of “knowledge” standing all alone, with no context and no actionable points. Hardly anything reminds me of it, so I won’t remember what’s in there, even when I most need it.

In contrast, my essays on this website form an Idea Bank for me. When I really need to solve a particular problem, I just need to go to my Bank and withdraw the relevant essay. When I’m ready and motivated, the essay will be there waiting for me. It’s highly actionable; it was written specifically with that in mind. The notes on my computer weren’t.

For these reasons, and for the others I mentioned a long time ago, I need to start writing essays once again. Enough of the pseudo-productive note-taking. It’s time for some real action.

I guess, what I’m trying to say is… I’m Back!
